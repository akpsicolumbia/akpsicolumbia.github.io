Our History

Founded in 1920, the Chi chapter of AKPsi is the oldest business fraternity at Columbia University. Our current members are involved in several organizations on campus and study a variety of different majors. Our alumni work at the top banks, consulting firms, and Fortune 500 companies. Some of them are enrolled at some of the nation’s most competitive graduate programs.

National History

Alpha Kappa Psi was founded in 1904 on the principles of educating both its members and the public to appreciate and demand higher ideals in business, among many other things. Its founders, Fredrick Leach, Howard Jefferson, Nathan Lane, Jr., and George Bergen, set forth a plan to perpetuate business education and a sense of brotherhood that had formed during their years at NYU. The nation’s first professional business fraternity, AKPsi developed and expanded rapidly in its early years, becoming an international organization on April 18, 1931. Over the years, AKPsi has grown to become the largest business fraternity in the world, with 332 chapters and over 275,000 initiated members across several countries. AKPsi alumni include presidents of countries, chief executive officers of leading firms, and federal judges.
